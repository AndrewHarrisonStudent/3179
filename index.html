What Conditions Cause Crashes?

Victorian Car Crash Reports (July 2013 - March 2019)

How many people are injured or killed?

One of the most important statistics about road accidents is that of injuries and fatalities. Ultimately, car crashes are unfortunate consequences of a multitude of factors that come with modern infrastructure. Since we aren't getting rid of motorvehicles anytime soon, it can prove useful to take a closer look at the factors surrounding these accidents, such that appropriate policies and action can be taken to minimise the state-wide impact (seen map above) and the unfortunate impact on life (seen right). As shown, more than half the people involved in accidents are either injured or killed.

Accident Type

One aspect we can inspect is that of accident types, which help to indicate whether one type of accident is more likely than the other. We can also take a look at the pattern over time, such as days in a week, to spot any consistencies that should be addressed. As seen to the left, there is an increased prevelance of car accidents during the work days, which slowly increase leading into Friday. The most common accident appears to be vehicle on vehicle collisions throughout the week.

Road Geometry

Road Geometry is an interesting attribute to inspect, due to the focus from many Victorian officials in charge of road safety advocating for speed cameras at intersections to minimise the risk of accidents. Although we cannot draw significant conclusions from this analysis, it is easy to see that more than half the road accidents, from 2013-2019, did not occur at an intersection. This result may be due to the fact that the speed cameras deter reckless driving at important intersection types, such as 'Cross' and 'T', however, it appears more could be done to minimise accidents on regular streets.

Alcohol Related

Drink-Driving is a known issue, and it may appear that past campaigns have of made a strong impact on minimising it's occurance. Regardless of alcohol, however, more accidents occur at school pick-up time (3PM-4PM) and peak-hour traffic times (8:45AM, and 5:30PM), than at any other point in the day.

Light Conditions

Visibility is the most important factor surround a driver's capabilities to operate a motorvehicle. It may appear counterintuitive then, that more accidents occur during the day, than at dusk, dawn, or night. This can possibly be explained by the higher proportion of cars being present on the road throughout the day, leading to a greater risk of accidents occuring, which is reflected in these figures. Hence, it is easy to see, that although we may have assumptions on what factors may appear to be detrimental to drivers, it is only by looking at the data we can uncover the true detail.
